Cryptology Quest: Game Design

>

Our game is called "Crptograhpy Quest" and it is an interactive baord game that uses Crryptograhpy to move forward whilst trying to escape a monster (a rubber ducky in our case). It uses Caesar Cipher and key word shift to decrypt messages throughout the game. Each message is hand decrypted with no devices and has a 30 second time limit. Players can escape a run in with the monster by decrypting a challenge card. Our challenges in creating the game came with time. We did not have enopught time to create all of the encryptions that we wanted, meaning that we did not have enough moves to win the game. We took time while testing the game to troubshoot the game with Laurie. She helped us understand the particality of the game and how to make it competitive or fun depending on the player/team. If we had more time to work on the game I would suggest that we have a higher quality set up, along with more complex encryptions. I would suggest a timer to make the game more intense for players that enjoy that. Overall, i really enjoyed the game and think that it was well done for the time that was given.

Maureen Baginski

Crytology Research Blog

July 18 2023 Katharine Batista

Maureen Baginski began working as a cryptologist as a Russian language instructor in 1979 in Russia, the NSA during 9/11, and the FBI.

Baginski was involved in the 9/11 terriost as she was able to guide the actions of the NSA directly following the attacks.

She worked for The National Security Operations Center and the Signals Intelligence (SIGINT) as an officer for many years as a cryptologist. However, she was trasnferred to the FBI where she became the director of Intelligence from 2003-2005. After this she accpeted a position to work as a senior intelligence executive within the FBI.

Baginski was President of National Security Systems at SPARTA after retiring from the FBI, along with several other companies.
